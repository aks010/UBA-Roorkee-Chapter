Cattle Vaccination
  • Unnat Bharat Abhiyan, IIT Roorkee organized a Cattle Vaccination programme in Puranpur Village of Haridwar District on October 20, 2018. The aim of the programme was the vaccination of cattle against Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) which may affect their health severely. Animals with FMD typically have fever and blisters on tongue and lips, in and around the mouth, and on mammary glands and around the hooves. FMD apart from being harmful to animals, also cause production loss and hardship for farmers and ranchers.
  • This initiative was a successful attempt to promote cattle farming in the village of Puranpur by providing free vaccination for cattle in the village for FMD. The team collaborated with the government veterinary hospital in Bahadarabad for the vaccination drive.
    The doctor informed the team that as per a government scheme, the cattle are being vaccinated against FMD at the cost of Rs.2/- per vaccine. However, due to lack of awareness against the scheme, very few of the cattle owners vaccinate their cattle. So the team decided to carry out a vaccination drive against FMD the cost of which will not be taken by the villagers.
  • As per the availability of the doctor, the vaccinated drive was carried out on October 20, 2018 for which the promotions were done on October 13, 2018 and October 19, 2018. The drive was a huge success as 476 cattle were vaccinated in the village.
  • The vaccination initiative was carried out successfully and hopefully this would curb the spread of contagious diseases. The veterinary doctor was very impressed with the team and suggested to carry out a cattle health checkup camp in collaboration with team UBA. Another vaccination drive was successfully carried out in the village of Meerpur with the help of government veterinary hospital, Bahadrabad.
