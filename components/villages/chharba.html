Chharba

Vikasnagar block, Dehradun (Uttrakhand)

Chharba is a large village located in Vikasnagar Tehsil of Dehradun district, Uttarakhand with total 1425 families residing. The Chharba village has population of 7268 of which 3790 are males while 3478 are females as per Population Census 2011. 

In Chharba village population of children with age 0-6 is 1013 which makes up 13.94 % of total population of village. Average Sex Ratio of Chharba village is 918 which is lower than Uttarakhand state average of 963. Child Sex Ratio for the Chharba as per census is 890, equal than Uttarakhand average of 890. 

Chharba village has lower literacy rate compared to Uttarakhand. In 2011, literacy rate of Chharba village was 76.80 % compared to 78.82 % of Uttarakhand. In Chharba Male literacy stands at 82.73 % while female literacy rate was 70.38 %. 

As per constitution of India and Panchyati Raaj Act, Chharba village is administrated by Sarpanch (Head of Village) who is elected representative of village.

Schedule Caste (SC) constitutes 5.78 % while Schedule Tribe (ST) were 0.28 % of total population in Chharba village.

In Chharba village out of total population, 1935 were engaged in work activities. 71.37 % of workers describe their work as Main Work (Employment or Earning more than 6 Months) while 28.63 % were involved in Marginal activity providing livelihood for less than 6 months. Of 1935 workers engaged in Main Work, 410 were cultivators (owner or co-owner) while 192 were Agricultural labourer.
